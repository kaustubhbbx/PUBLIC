FAQ

Common Questions

Is this actually 100% free? No hidden cost? ▼
Yes. Microsoft covers 100% of the exam cost through a discount voucher code. No credit card is required at any stage. The voucher is applied when booking your exam through the Microsoft certification portal.
Why do I need to log into Microsoft Learn first? ▼
Microsoft tracks your module completions, credential progress, and plan participation using your signed-in account. If you're not logged in, none of your progress gets saved and your application cannot be verified. Log in once at learn.microsoft.com and all links will work correctly.
How long does voucher approval take for Data Days? ▼
Usually a few days after submitting. Check your inbox and your Spam / Promotions folder. The sender will be a Microsoft or Pearson VUE email address.
Can I apply for more than one exam? ▼
The Data Days form lets you select one exam per submission. Focus on the one most relevant to your current goals. For the AI Sweepstakes, completing multiple Applied Skills still only counts as one entry.
Do I need to finish the modules before applying for Data Days? ▼
No. The form asks which modules you have completed or plan to complete. Write the ones you intend to do — you can start them after submitting the application.
What is the difference between Data Days and the AI Sweepstakes? ▼
Data Days is a direct application for a voucher — fill a form and Microsoft may send you one for DP-600, DP-700, or DP-800. The AI Sweepstakes requires first completing a free Applied Skills credential, then submitting an entry. Winners are selected from a pool — 900 vouchers total for AB-100, AB-900, or AB-620 exams. Both campaigns are free to participate in.
What if Data Days vouchers are already out? ▼
Try it anyway — the form is still worth filling. If Data Days vouchers are exhausted, the AI Sweepstakes is still open until July 15 with 900 vouchers. And the free Applied Skills credentials are always available regardless of either campaign.
