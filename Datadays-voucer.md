## 📌 What Is This?

Microsoft's **Data Days 2026** campaign is offering **free certification exam vouchers** for three high-demand Microsoft Fabric and SQL exams. There is no catch — you fill a short eligibility form and Microsoft sends you a discount code covering 100% of the exam cost.

This guide walks you through **every single field** of that form so you don't get stuck or make a mistake.

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Open the Voucher Form

Click the link below. It opens the official Microsoft Community page.

**👉 [Click here to open the form](https://community.fabric.microsoft.com/t5/custom/page/page-id/campaign_form?campaignID=Y2FtcGFpZ24tMTc4MTA0MDI5MDIxMw==&wt.mc_id=studentamb_519583)**

Once the page loads:
1. Scroll down past the session schedule
2. Find and click **"Get Certified for FREE"**
3. The application form will appear — follow this guide to fill it

## 🎯 Step 2 — Choose Your Exam

Pick **one** of the three certifications below. Read the descriptions and choose the one that matches your background.

| | Exam | Role | Best For You If... |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔵 | **DP-600** | Fabric Analytics Engineer | You work with Power BI, dashboards, or business reporting |
| 🟣 | **DP-700** | Fabric Data Engineer | You love coding, data pipelines, and engineering systems |
| 🟠 | **DP-800** | SQL AI Engineer | You work with SQL databases and want to add AI skills |

> 💡 **Not sure?** Go with **DP-600** — it is the most beginner-friendly and has the most study resources available.

## ❓ FAQ

**Is this actually free?**
Yes. Microsoft covers 100% of the exam cost through a discount voucher. No card required.

**How long does approval take?**
Usually a few days. Check your email including spam/promotions folders.

**Can I apply for more than one exam?**
The form lets you pick one. Apply for the one most relevant to you right now.

**What if vouchers run out?**
They are limited and first-come first-served. Apply as soon as possible.

**Do I need to finish the modules before applying?**
No. The form asks what you have done or *plan* to do. You can start studying after applying.
